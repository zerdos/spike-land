# How to Take Care of Pixel and Mad Max — A Complete Guide

## Who Are These Dogs?

**Pixel** is a 1.5-year-old Golden Retriever. She's an adolescent — past the
crazy puppy phase but still full of energy. She knows the basics but needs
consistent reinforcement. She's gentle, social, and food-motivated.

**Mad Max** is a 4-month-old black Golden Retriever and Labrador mix. He's a
baby. Everything is new to him. He's learning bite inhibition, house training,
and how to exist in the world. He will chew everything. This is normal.

## Daily Routine

### Morning
1. Wake up — take both dogs outside immediately for toilet. Mad Max especially
   cannot hold it long.
2. Feed Mad Max his breakfast (puppy food, dry kibble, amount per package
   instructions for his weight).
3. Feed Pixel her breakfast (adult food, dry kibble, amount per package
   instructions for her weight).
4. Fresh water in both bowls.
5. Morning walk: Pixel gets 30 minutes, Mad Max gets 15-20 minutes. Both on
   lead.

### Midday
1. Take Mad Max out for toilet — at 4 months he needs to go every 3-4 hours.
2. Mad Max gets a small midday meal (puppy food, third portion).
3. Pixel does not need a midday meal.
4. Short play session in the house or garden.

### Evening
1. Evening walk: same as morning. Pixel 30 min, Mad Max 15-20 min.
2. Feed both dogs dinner.
3. Fresh water.
4. Final toilet trip before bed.
5. Mad Max may need one more toilet trip during the night or very early morning.

## Feeding Rules

- Pixel eats **adult** dry food. Mad Max eats **puppy** dry food. They are
  different formulas — do not mix them up.
- Mad Max eats 3 times a day (morning, midday, evening). Pixel eats 2 times
  (morning, evening).
- Always have fresh water available.
- Never give human food. Especially dangerous: chocolate, grapes, raisins,
  onions, garlic, xylitol (sweetener in gum).
- If you're unsure how much to feed, read the back of the food bag — it has a
  chart based on weight.

## How to Communicate With Dogs — The Loop Theory

Dogs live in feedback loops. Every interaction teaches them something. Here's
how it works:

### The Positive Loop
Dog does something good (sits, comes when called, stays calm) → You reward with
a treat or happy voice → Dog learns "this behaviour gets me good things" → Dog
repeats the behaviour.

This is the only training method you need. Reward what you want to see more of.

### The Ignore Loop
Dog does something unwanted (jumps up, barks for attention, steals a shoe) →
You turn away, say nothing, give zero attention → Dog learns "this behaviour
gets me nothing" → Dog stops doing it.

This requires patience. The dog will try harder at first (this is called an
"extinction burst" — it gets worse before it gets better). Stay calm. It works.

### The Dangerous Loop — Shouting
Dog barks → Human shouts "STOP!" → Dog hears excitement and barks more → Human
shouts louder → Dog barks louder → Infinite loop.

Shouting is never the answer. The dog does not understand words. The dog
understands energy. Calm energy = calm dog. Excited energy = excited dog.

### The Calm Loop
You are calm → Dog mirrors your calm → You stay calm → Dog stays calm → Peace.

This is the most important loop. Your emotional state IS the dog's environment.
If you are stressed, the dog is stressed. If you are relaxed, the dog is
relaxed.

### Puppy Biting (Mad Max Specific)
At 4 months, Mad Max will bite your hands during play. This is completely
normal — he is learning bite inhibition.

The response: say "OW" in a sharp voice (not angry, just surprised), then turn
away for 3 seconds. Do not engage. Then resume play.

The loop: bite → play stops → puppy learns "biting ends the fun" → puppy bites
softer → eventually stops.

Never hit a puppy for biting. Never hold their mouth shut. This creates fear,
not learning.

## Walking Rules

- Both dogs on lead. Always.
- Mad Max is a puppy — his joints are developing. No running on hard surfaces.
  15-20 minute walks maximum.
- Pixel needs more exercise — 30 minutes minimum, twice a day. She will get
  destructive if under-exercised.
- Let them sniff. Sniffing is how dogs process the world. A walk where the dog
  gets to sniff is more tiring than a fast walk with no sniffing.
- If another dog approaches and you're unsure, put yourself between your dog
  and the other dog. Stay calm.

## What NOT to Do

1. **Never shout at the dogs.** They don't understand words. They understand
   tone and energy.
2. **Never hit or physically punish.** This creates fear and breaks trust
   permanently.
3. **Never leave Mad Max and Pixel unsupervised together for long periods.**
   Mad Max is small. Pixel is bigger. Accidents happen even with gentle dogs.
4. **Never feed human food.** Especially chocolate, grapes, onions.
5. **Never skip toilet breaks for Mad Max.** He's 4 months old. He physically
   cannot hold it. If he has an accident inside, clean it up quietly. No
   punishment — he doesn't understand.

## The Most Important Thing

Dogs are simple. They need:
1. Food and water on schedule
2. Toilet breaks on schedule
3. Exercise
4. Calm, consistent energy from their human

That's it. If you do these four things, the dogs will be happy, healthy, and
calm. You don't need to be perfect. You just need to be consistent and kind.

The dogs don't judge you. They don't care about your past. They care about
right now — are you calm, are you present, is there food in the bowl, and do
you want to go for a walk?

That's the whole thing. That's enough.
