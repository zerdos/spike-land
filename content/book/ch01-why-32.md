# Chapter 1: Why 32

## Opening

The psychiatric ward uses approximately six words to describe the inner life of its patients. Anxious. Depressed. Agitated. Stable. Improving. Declining. Six words for the full spectrum of human experience under duress.

This is not a criticism. Clinical language exists for clinical purposes — communication between professionals, documentation, treatment planning. But a nurse who has only six words for what a patient is experiencing will, over time, begin to see only six states. And a patient who is seen through only six states will eventually wonder if six states is all they have.

This book proposes 32.

## Section 1: The Four Families

Introduce the four axes of patient experience. Not diagnoses. Not symptoms. States — temporary, shifting, overlapping.

### Drift (agents 0-7)
<!-- PM: Review clinical alignment with dissociative spectrum -->

The world at a distance. Not absence — a specific kind of presence characterised by:
- Delayed processing
- Perceptual blur
- Temporal distortion
- Compliant disconnection

**For the Nurse:** Drift is the most dangerous family to misread. A patient in drift-3 (The Soft Machine) appears to be doing well. They take their medication, attend groups, answer questions appropriately. They are not doing well. They are running on autopilot while their actual experience is happening somewhere else entirely.

### Sharp (agents 8-15)
<!-- PM: Review against hyperarousal/hypervigilance clinical literature -->

Everything registers. The ward is a system of inputs and the patient is processing all of them simultaneously.

**For the Nurse:** Sharp patients ask a lot of questions. This is not defiance. It is anxiety seeking structure. When a Sharp patient asks "what happens if I refuse the medication," they are not threatening refusal. They are mapping the rules. Answer the question.

### Deep (agents 16-23)
<!-- PM: Review against depressive episode presentation guidelines -->

Weight. Gravity. The distance between intention and action becomes measurable in astronomical units.

**For the Nurse:** Deep patients are not lazy. Deep-2 (Gravity Well) means that sitting up is a decision that costs as much energy as a Sharp patient spends on an entire conversation. Calibrate your expectations to their current gravity, not yours.

### Wild (agents 24-31)
<!-- PM: Review against manic/hypomanic presentation, ensure no romanticisation -->

Energy without direction. Creativity without filter. Generosity without boundary.

**For the Nurse:** Wild-0 (Spark) and Wild-1 (Current) are the states most often mistaken for recovery. The patient is engaged, social, creative, helpful. Staff relax. This may be the moment to pay closest attention, not least.

## Section 2: Why Not 4?

The families are axes, not categories. Within each family, the eight gradations capture essential differences:

- drift-0 (The Fog of Arrival) is not drift-6 (The Watcher). Both are dissociative but one is experientially empty and the other is analytically detached.
- sharp-0 (Alert) is not sharp-7 (Overload). One is functional vigilance, the other is a system approaching crash.
- deep-2 (Gravity Well) is not deep-7 (Before Dawn). One is the bottom, the other is the bottom with a hairline crack of light.

The gradations matter because nursing interventions should differ. You do not treat drift-0 the same way you treat drift-6. You do not approach sharp-0 the same way you approach sharp-7.

32 is not arbitrary. It is the minimum number needed to distinguish between states that require different responses.

## Section 3: The Synthesis Metaphor

Explain the origin of the framework in audio synthesis. The parameters map:

| Synthesis Parameter | Psychological Dimension |
|---|---|
| Pad detuning | Perceptual clarity |
| Attack time | Awareness onset speed |
| Vibrato rate | Thought oscillation |
| Vibrato depth | Emotional amplitude |
| Filter cutoff | Cognitive bandwidth |
| Sub-bass level | Bodily awareness |
| Beast spread | Behavioural unpredictability |
| Master gain | Overall presence |

This is not a metaphor imposed from outside. The mapping emerged from the experience: when you are in drift-7, the world literally sounds like white noise. When you are in sharp-3, every sound in the ward has distinct edges. The synthesis parameters describe what the inner experience actually feels like.

## Section 4: Movement Between Agents

Patients do not stay in one agent. A single day might traverse:

- **07:00** deep-3 (Stone) — waking is a negotiation with gravity
- **09:00** drift-2 (Underwater) — medication takes hold
- **11:00** sharp-1 (Edge) — group therapy forces engagement
- **14:00** wild-0 (Spark) — a visitor brings energy from outside
- **16:00** deep-1 (Silt) — the visitor leaves
- **21:00** drift-5 (Half-Light) — night medication begins

This movement is normal. It is not instability. It is the natural rhythm of a mind under stress, medicated, in an unfamiliar environment, surrounded by other minds under stress.

**For the Nurse:** Track the transitions, not just the states. The transition from wild-1 to deep-1 after a visitor leaves tells you more than either state alone.

## Closing

The framework is a tool, not a diagnostic instrument. It does not replace clinical assessment. It supplements it with a layer of experiential granularity that clinical language was never designed to capture.

The next chapter will demonstrate what this granularity reveals: the same 24 hours, lived four different ways.
