## Part I: The Framework

### Chapter 1 — Why 32
*Introducing the four families of patient experience*

The Radix agent model: four axes (Drift, Sharp, Deep, Wild), eight gradations each. Why granularity matters in psychiatric observation. Why "depressed" and "anxious" are not enough. How the same patient moves between agents across a single day.

- Agents featured: overview of all 32
- Word target: 5,000
- File: `ch01-why-32.md`

### Chapter 2 — The Same Ward, 32 Times
*One day, four lenses*

A single 24-hour period on the ward narrated four times — once through each family's representative agent. Same events, same staff, same meals, same medication round. Radically different experiences.

- Agents featured: drift-0, sharp-3, deep-2, wild-5
- Word target: 6,000
- File: `ch02-same-ward.md`

## Part II: Ward Life

### Chapter 3 — Admission
*The first 72 hours*

Intake paperwork. Belongings in a plastic bag. The locked door. First medication. First night with the lights that never fully go off. What nurses say vs. what patients hear.

- Agents featured: drift-0 (fog of arrival), sharp-3 (hypervigilant scanning)
- Word target: 5,000
- File: `ch03-admission.md`

### Chapter 4 — The Middle
*Days that repeat*

The medication queue at 8am. Group therapy where nobody wants to be. Meals at fixed times. The courtyard. Visiting hours. The specific weight of institutional boredom. Time moving differently.

- Agents featured: deep-2 (gravity of routine), wild-5 (restless against structure)
- Word target: 5,000
- File: `ch04-the-middle.md`

### Chapter 5 — Discharge and After
*The gap between the ward and the world*

Discharge planning that happens to you. Medication education at maximum cognitive load. Walking out. The first supermarket. The first night alone. The follow-up appointment three weeks away.

- Agents featured: sharp-7 (information overload), drift-4 (re-entry fog)
- Word target: 5,000
- File: `ch05-discharge.md`
