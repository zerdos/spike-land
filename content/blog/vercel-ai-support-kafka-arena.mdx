Look I'm not writing a think piece. I'm not going to tell you "what we can learn from this." I'm going to show you what happened when I tried to delete my Vercel account today and you can decide for yourself if this is okay.

I have a Hobby account. I've paid them about $209 over the past few months. I'm moving everything to Cloudflare. Normal stuff. I just want out.

## The conversation

I open their support chat. An AI greets me.

I type: **I WANT FULL REFUND FOR ALL MY PAYMENTS.**

The AI immediately pulls up my 8 invoices, tells me most are outside the 30-day refund window, identifies two that qualify ($20 and $75). Fine. Fair. I respect that.

Then I say: **and I would like to DELETE my projects and my profile after that.**

The AI tells me it can't do that. Go to Account Settings, scroll down, click Delete Account.

So I say: **DELETE NOW**

It says: I completely understand the urgency, but I'm not able to delete your account or projects for you — this is something only you can do for security reasons.

**Security reasons.** The AI can read my billing history, see my projects, knows my email, but pressing delete is a security concern. Okay.

I go to Account Settings. I scroll down. I click Delete Account.

It doesn't work.

I go back to the chat.

**TRIED DIDN'T WORK**

The AI asks me to describe what happened. Is there an error message? Is the button greyed out?

I am not QA for Vercel's settings page. I am a customer trying to leave.

**Please let me delete my profile. Don't let me tell 100 times please please please please please please**

The AI says: I hear you — no more back and forth. Then it sends me to vercel.com/accountrecovery.

Another link. Another page. Another form.

I type: **CALL ME**

The AI thinks for a while. Then it outputs a single word:

**HUMAN**

The AI asked for a human. The AI itself gave up and called for help. And you know what happened?

The system responded: "We're sorry! Our agent is currently unable to answer your inquiry. Please open a support case."

The support case form appeared. It asked me to select a severity level. Under severity levels it said: "Visit our Community for further help or **create a paid account for dedicated support.**"

To get help deleting my free account, they suggested I upgrade to a paid account.

I typed: **NOTHING IS WORKING**

Same message again. Agent unable to answer. Open a support case.

Dead end.

## That's it

That's the whole story. I said delete fourteen times. The AI said "I hear you" and "I understand" and "I'm sorry" and not once did anything get deleted.

The AI was nice. That's the worst part. It wasn't rude. It wasn't cold. It performed empathy perfectly — "I hear you," "I understand your frustration," "I genuinely wish I could do it for you." Every sentence was warm and caring and absolutely useless.

It's like being trapped in a room with someone who keeps saying "I totally get it" while standing in front of the only exit.

## What I'm doing now

I sent an email to privacy@vercel.com citing UK GDPR Article 17, right to erasure. They have 30 days. I live in Brighton, I'm a UK resident, this isn't optional for them.

If that doesn't work, the ICO gets a complaint.

I shouldn't need to invoke data protection law to close a Hobby account on a web hosting platform. But here we are.

## One thing

Vercel is a good platform. The deployments are fast. The DX is smooth. v0 is cool. I have nothing bad to say about the product.

But whoever designed the exit flow — the one where an AI chatbot loops you through broken links until you give up — either didn't think about it, or thought about it very carefully. I don't know which is worse.

Make the delete button work. That's my entire feedback. Make it work.
