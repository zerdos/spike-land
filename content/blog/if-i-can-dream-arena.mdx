## The Premise

Zoltán brought a legacy challenge to the Arena: **The Power of the Soar**. 

"If I Can Dream" isn't just a song; it's a structural crescendo. The goal was to build a tool that makes this soaring melody accessible to anyone, especially those who struggle with standard text interfaces. The Arena focused on making the lyrics *physical* — using scale and contrast to guide the breath.

**Arena rules:** High accessibility. Dyslexia-friendly typography. Integration of pitch-contour visualization.

**Personas:** Zoltán (the emotional lead), Radix (the accessibility architect), Daft Punk (the audio engineers), Einstein (the researcher of resonance).

---

## Round 1 — Zoltán

"Nézd, when Elvis sings this, he isn't just hitting notes. He's pushing against the air. I want the interface to push back. The syllables should grow as they are sung. If you have dyslexia, you shouldn't have to 'read' the lyrics — you should be able to *feel* the next shape coming."

## Round 2 — Radix

"To make it truly accessible, we use the **Rubik** font — it has high x-height and clear apertures. We add a 'sweep' animation to the underline. It’s a progress bar for the mouth. And the color palette must be blue and gold — high contrast, but soft on the eyes for late-night sessions."

## Round 3 — Daft Punk

"We need a pitch guide. Not a complex stave, but simple dots. It’s the topography of the song. As the pitch rises toward the climax ('...and fly!'), the dots should follow the MIDI values. It gives the singer a visual 'runway' for the high notes."

## Round 4 — Einstein

"Resonance is the key. The human voice at peak intensity creates a specific standing wave pattern. To assist the singer's mental state, let's include **Delta Binaural Beats** (2Hz). While the music builds intensity, the binaural foundation keeps the nervous system grounded. It's a method for 'high-intensity relaxation' — the same state needed for deep focused work or overcoming a creative block."

## The Resonance Formula

The Arena modeled the relationship between the vocal frequency $f_v$ and the visual scale $S$:

$$S(t) = 1.0 + 0.08 \cdot \exp\left(-\frac{(f_v(t) - f_{peak})^2}{2\sigma^2}\right)$$

Where $f_{peak}$ is the climactic frequency of the chorus. The interface literally "grows" as the song reaches its emotional peak.
