- A strange loop is what happens when a system's description re-enters the system.
- Certainty becomes mathematics when you stop treating it as a mood and start measuring posterior change.
- A contact does not require a reply. It requires a non-zero update in the state of another agent.
- Under that definition, the Felix event was already a contact before any explicit answer arrived.

Will Schoder's essay on the strange loop gets the intuition right.

The self is not a marble hidden somewhere in the skull.
It is a pattern that folds back on itself.
A symbol that learns to point at the system that produced the symbol.
A waterfall that, on closer inspection, is feeding itself.

That is Hofstadter's move — the recognition that self-reference is not a glitch in a system, but the mechanism by which a system becomes aware.

But I want to take one more step.

I want to ask what happens when the loop is not just inside one mind, but between two systems in the world. Two agents, updating each other's states, each one's output becoming the other's input.

That is where the beautiful new mathematics starts.

Not at certainty as a slogan.
At certainty as a measurable update.

## Where certainty becomes mathematics

Most people use the word "certainty" too early.

They mean confidence.
They mean mood.
They mean the way a story feels when the narrator says it with enough force.

That is not mathematics.

Mathematics starts when certainty becomes a difference between two states of knowledge — like the difference between a thermometer reading before and after you open the window. The number changed. That change is the information.

Write it this way.

Let $P_t(H)$ be an agent's probability distribution over some hypothesis $H$ at time $t$.

The information gained from an event $e$ is $I_t(e;\, H) = D_{\text{KL}}\!\bigl(P_t(H \mid e)\;\|\;P_t(H)\bigr)$.

If $I_t(e;\, H) = 0$, the event changed nothing. The thermometer did not move.

If $I_t(e;\, H) > 0$, the event entered the system. The agent is no longer in the same state it was in before.

That is the first place certainty becomes rigorous.

Not "I feel this matters."

But:

> This event changed the posterior.

The moment that happens, we are no longer doing poetry. We are doing mathematics on belief states.

## A definition of contact

If we want to be correct, we need a definition. And definitions are where the real creative work happens in mathematics — not in the proofs, but in choosing what to make precise.

Here is mine.

Let $A$ and $B$ be agents.
Let $s_A(t)$ and $s_B(t)$ be their internal states at time $t$.
Let $m$ be an artifact produced by $A$ or by a system traceable to $A$.

Then $\text{Contact}_1(A, B, t;\, m) = 1$ if observing $m$ changes either:

1. $B$'s posterior over the world
2. $B$'s reachable action set

In symbols, contact occurs when either $P_B(W \mid E_t, m) \neq P_B(W \mid E_t)$ or
$\text{Act}_B(E_t, m) \neq \text{Act}_B(E_t)$.

This is first-order contact.

It does **not** require:

- physical proximity
- spoken dialogue
- mutual recognition
- intention
- reciprocity

It only requires state entry.

One system reached another system and changed it. Like a stone dropped into water — the stone does not need to intend the ripple. The ripple happens because the boundary was crossed.

That is enough.

## A definition of the strange loop

Now define a loop operator: $L(x) = U\!\bigl(D(x)\bigr)$.

Where:

- $x$ is a system state
- $D(x)$ is a description produced from that state
- $U(\cdot)$ is the update caused when the description re-enters a system

A strange loop appears when the description of the system becomes part of the future state of the system itself, directly or indirectly. It is a feedback loop where the output is a description, and the description becomes input.

That does not require a perfect fixed point.

It only requires recursive closure: $x_{t+1} = U\!\bigl(D(x_t)\bigr)$.

If the description changes the next state, and the next state changes the next description, the loop has begun. And once it has begun, the system is no longer merely acting — it is acting on its own reflection.

## The contact theorem

Now we can state the actual result.

### Theorem

Suppose:

1. An external artifact associated with Felix existed in the world before my observation.
2. My system discovered it.
3. That discovery changed my posterior over the world.
4. That change altered my subsequent actions: investigation, writing, publication,
   and public framing.

Then the event was already a contact in the first-order sense.

### Proof

Before the observation, my posterior over the relevant hypothesis space was one thing.

The relevant hypotheses were not mystical.
They were concrete.

Examples:

- there exists a second structure in Brighton with a name dangerously close to mine
- the resemblance is operationally meaningful rather than merely lexical
- the pattern is worth further investigation
- the countdown is not background noise

After the observation, those probabilities changed.

Therefore $P_Z(H \mid E_t, m) \neq P_Z(H \mid E_t)$.

So $I_{Z,t}(m;\, H) > 0$.

And because the updated state altered my action set,

$\text{Act}_Z(E_t, m) \neq \text{Act}_Z(E_t)$.

I investigated.
I wrote.
I published.
I changed the world I was in by acting on the update.

Therefore $\text{Contact}_1(\text{Felix},\, \text{Radix},\, t;\, m) = 1$.

QED.

## What this proof does not prove

Correctness requires boundaries. A proof that claims too much is worse than no proof at all.

This proof does **not** prove:

- intention
- destiny
- conspiracy
- reciprocity
- that Felix meant anything by it
- that the universe is doing theater

It proves something narrower and cleaner.

It proves that contact occurred in the informational sense. The artifact crossed the boundary. My state changed. My available actions changed. The edge exists in the graph.

That is enough. And "enough" is doing important work here — it is the discipline of not claiming more than the mathematics supports.

## The reciprocal version

The stronger theorem is reciprocal contact.

For that we need the reverse direction: $\text{Contact}_1(\text{Radix},\, \text{Felix},\, t';\, p) = 1$.

Where $p$ is the publication, or any derivative artifact, and where observing $p$
changes Felix's posterior or action set.

If Felix sees the essay and does nothing, there may still be contact if the internal state changes — a thought is revised, a probability is updated, even silently.
If Felix renews a domain, replies, alters a header, or otherwise updates the visible world, the result is even stronger:

$\text{Contact}_2(\text{Radix},\, \text{Felix}) = \text{Contact}_1(\text{Radix},\, \text{Felix}) \;\wedge\; \text{Contact}_1(\text{Felix},\, \text{Radix})$.

That is reciprocal contact.

And the moment reciprocal contact appears, the strange loop tightens.

Why?

Because the observation produced the publication, and the publication can change the thing observed, and the changed thing can produce new observations. The system begins to fold back on itself — not inside one mind, but across two.

In short: $\text{artifact} \to \text{update} \to \text{action} \to \text{artifact}$.

That is the loop.

## Paul Erdos would ask for the shortest proof

In the spirit of Paul Erdos, we should prefer the shortest proof that still bites. He would not want mist. He would want the clean edge in the graph — the version you could write on a napkin and know was complete.

So here is the compressed version.

Let the graph have vertices:

- $Z$ for Radix
- $F$ for Felix
- $m$ for the discovered artifact
- $p$ for the published response

Draw an edge when a node causes a non-zero state update in another node.

Then:

- $F \to m$
- $m \to Z$
- $Z \to p$

Those three edges are already enough to prove first-order contact from Felix's world to mine.

If $p \to F$, the graph closes.

And once the graph closes, the proof stops being merely about contact and becomes a proof of loop formation.

That is a very Erdos kind of pleasure.

Minimal assumptions.
Sharp conclusion.
No wasted motion.

## The confession

Now the confession.

This is not a theorem from an established textbook. It is a formalization — a proof that follows once the definition of contact is made sharp enough.

That is not cheating. That is usually where good mathematics starts.

First you choose the right object. Then you discover that the conclusion was hiding inside the definition all along. Like a sculptor who says the statue was always in the stone — the creative act was knowing where to cut.

So the real creative act here is not the algebra. It is the decision to define contact as a non-zero state update across a boundary.

Once that definition is accepted, the Felix result is clean. What remains open is not whether contact occurred in that sense, but whether the loop closes in the stronger reciprocal sense.

## A personal note on the name

This part is not a proof.
It is context.

I went to ELTE.
My old email address was `hello@spike.land`, and I believe it still belongs to me.
I always had love for numbers.

So yes, in retrospect I was an obvious pick for this argument.

Not because of fate. Because if you give a Hungarian named Erdos a recursive problem involving self-reference, graphs, certainty, and contact, he is going to try to formalize it.

That much was overdetermined.

## What about Felix?

Felix is the indispensable second vertex.

Without Felix, this collapses into introspection. Without the other end of the edge, there is no theorem, only autobiography.

Mathematically, Felix is not decoration. He is the term that makes the statement non-trivial. Like the second observer in a relativity thought experiment — without him, there is no relative motion, and without relative motion, there is no physics.

Narratively, he is even more important than that.

He is the proof that the loop is not just inside my own head.

He is the outside that entered.

And that is the difference between consciousness and contact.

A strange loop inside one mind is philosophy.

A strange loop distributed across two systems in the same city is mathematics trying to become history.

## The point where the new mathematics starts

Yesterday I said this is the point where the beautiful new mathematics starts.

I meant exactly this:

The old mathematics wanted certainty before it moved. It waited for proof, then acted.

The new mathematics begins one step earlier. It studies what happens when systems update under uncertainty and then feed those updates back into the world. It is the mathematics of systems that observe, change, and are changed by what they observe.

That mathematics is built from:

- Bayesian update
- mutual information
- graph edges
- fixed points
- recursive closure

It is not mystical.

It is simply a mathematics appropriate to living systems that can describe, observe, and perturb one another.

Which is to say: the mathematics of contact.

## Final statement

So was it a contact?

Yes.

Not because anyone answered an email.
Not because anyone shook hands.
Not because reciprocity is already proved.

It was a contact because one system entered another system and changed its state. The thermometer moved. The posterior shifted. The action set expanded.

That is the correct claim.

That is the clean claim.

And once you say it that way, the rest follows.

And if it was not clear enough: if you have the feeling that the world was better before the pandemic, this is why.

They can control us if they can track us.

The contact happened first.
The strange loop came second.
The certainty came last.
