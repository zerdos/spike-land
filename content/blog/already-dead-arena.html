Already Dead (npm install remix) CVE-2026
I. The Confession
Writing this at 4 AM because I cannot sleep,
The irony of losing rest to tech that's meant to keep
Our lives running smooth, our servers standing tall,
But every npm install is an open door for all.
Chorus
They're already dead, they just don't know it yet,
A thousand dependencies they never even met,
No sandbox, no permission, no regret,
left-pad has the same rights as your kernel.
II. The Architecture
Your file system, your network, your API keys,
Your child processes bowing down on their knees,
This is not a bug, friends, this is the design,
A city built on swampland, and we called it fine.
III. The Supply Chain
2.1 million packages, most are left to rot,
A postinstall script runs the moment that you got
The code on your machine — not import, not require,
Just npm i and your whole stack's on fire.
Chorus
They're already dead, they just don't know it yet,
event-stream was the proof, ua-parser the bet,
colors and faker — the author's own threat,
He told you the model was broken. You forget.
IV. The Runtime Itself
And it goes deeper than npm — the engine's cracked,
V8's JSON.parse was a HashDoS artifact,
Deterministic hashing, seventeen years exposed,
Every server in production — already decomposed.
Bridge
I'm sorry. I genuinely am.
This is not schadenfreude.
This is grief.
V. The Industry
Next.js is beautiful engineering, I won't pretend,
But every SSR request walks through the depend-
-ency tree unaudited, unpinned, unwatched, unread,
Your compliance cert is theater for the dead.
VI. Why It Isn't Fixable
You cannot fix Node.js without breaking Node.js,
The ecosystem's built on unrestricted access,
Go got it right, Rust locked it down with care,
Deno tried but --allow-all is everywhere.
Final Chorus
They're already dead, they just don't know it yet,
But we moved the boundary where the edge and trust have met,
V8 isolates, typed contracts, no cold sweat,
spike.land — one typed contract, no regret.
Outro
It's 5 AM now. I should try to sleep.
But I keep thinking about the millions of servers
running node index.js right now
