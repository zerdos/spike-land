# Let my body recover

---

## The thesis, in one sentence

**Either ban it nationally, or give it to me.**

There is no third way. What we have now — where the NHS prescribes it to me, but society treats it as a shame; where one step is legal and the next is a crime; where I have to explain myself even with the prescription in my pocket — this is hypocrisy made flesh.

---

## The facts, briefly

The NHS **prescribed it to me**. Medical cannabis. For sleep. For rest. So that my body can heal after what it has been through.

This is not street weed. This is not recreation. This is not a lifestyle. This is **medicine**, the same way my grandfather's blood pressure pill is medicine, the same way my friend's insulin is medicine. Prescription. Dose. Doctor's signature.

The difference: you don't have to be ashamed of the blood pressure pill. With medical cannabis, you do — at least that is what almost everything around me signals. The glances. The whispers. The "but really, you know, what it's for". Yes. I know. A doctor told me.

---

## What I use it for

I am not a junkie. I was sick. I went into hospital. I came out. My body — my ADHD, overclocked, never-resting brain — cannot sleep on its own. Not loops, **infinite loops**. Not thoughts, **attacks**.

Medical cannabis does this:

1. **Turns down the brain noise** enough for me to fall asleep.
2. **I sleep deep**, not waking every three hours.
3. **I am not a medication-fog zombie** in the morning, the way sleeping pills leave you.
4. **My body rests.** That is the only thing that matters right now.

This is not "high". This is **recovery**.

---

## The hypocrisy

Here is the problem, and I will say it out loud because no one else says it loud enough:

- **The state prescribes it** to me, through a medical system we all pay for with our taxes.
- **The same state** would punish someone who does exactly this without a prescription.
- **My neighbour**, who has the same symptoms as I do, but doesn't have an expensive private psychiatrist to write it out — he is a criminal if he tries the same.

This is not policy. This is **class filtering**. If you can afford the private consultant, it's medicine. If you can't, it's prison.

And this is not just my problem — it is **everyone's** problem, anyone who has ever slept badly, had anxiety, tried to get back on their feet after trauma.

---

## What I'm asking for

Not "liberalisation". Not "free weed". Not recreation.

**I am asking society for a choice.**

If you believe it is dangerous — **ban it completely**. Take it off the NHS. Take it from patients. Own the consequence: that people who are healing right now will relapse. That they will break without sleep. That the psychiatric wards will fill up. Own it.

Or:

**Give it** to everyone who has a medical need. Not only to the wealthy. Not only to the well-documented. Not only to those who know how to describe their symptoms in fluent English in a GP consultation.

**But stop doing what you do now**: prescribe it, and then shame the person for using it. Let me carry it home legally, but let the police question me if they smell it in the car. Let me "have a right to it", but let my employer still fire me. Give me a prescription, but let customs seize it at the border.

**This is not a system. It is a trap.**

---

## Let my body recover

My body went through something I would not wish on anyone. Hospital. Exhaustion. A&E. Psychiatric ward. My dogs spent four weeks with friends because I could not look after them.

Now I am healing. Slowly. With a lot of sleep. A lot of rest. A lot of silence.

Medical cannabis is one of the tools of that healing. Not the only one, not the most important — but **a tool**. It is like a cast on a broken leg: it does not remake the bone, it just **makes space** for it to knit itself back together.

**Let my body knit itself back together.**

Don't make it conditional on how much I can afford in private care. Don't shame me because it comes from a plant and not from a lab. Don't send the message that only the "deserving" are allowed to heal.

---

## Closing

This post is not a campaign. Not a petition. Not a policy paper.

This is a person, healing right now, saying out loud what a lot of people think but don't dare:

**Either ban it nationally, or give it to me.**

**Let my body recover.**

Thank you to my NHS doctor, for having the courage to write the prescription. Thank you to my friends, who took in my dogs. Thank you to Péter, Peti, everyone who did not turn away.

And to the rest — the ones still at "but really, do you need this?" — here is what I have:

*You don't have to understand. It's enough if you don't judge.*

---

**— Zoltán, Brighton, spring 2026**
