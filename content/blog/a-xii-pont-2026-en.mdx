# The Twelve Points — 2026

---

## Preface

On 15 March 1848, in Pest, at the Pilvax Coffeehouse, Sándor Petőfi and his circle wrote down — in **Twelve Points** — what the Hungarian nation demands. They did not ask. They **demanded.** They printed it, handed it out, took it to the streets, and that day they swore: *"We swear we shall be slaves no longer!"*

178 years later, on 12 April 2026, in a Brighton kitchen, beside an NHS prescription, an ADHD brain at three in the morning asks the same question — but not of the nation: **of the body.**

These are the Twelve Points. Not political. Not national. **Bodily.**

---

## What the Hungarian body demands

### 1. The right to sleep.

Not a luxury. Not a privilege. **A right.** Sleep is not performance, not work, not "efficient rest." Sleep is the body's basic need, like air. Whoever does not sleep, dies. Whoever half-sleeps, half-lives. The state must not regulate it, the employer must not punish it, society must not shame it.

### 2. The right to medicinal plants.

If the doctor prescribed it, it is medicine. Even if it came from a plant. Even if we have known it for 5,000 years. Even if the police have an opinion about it. Lock-and-key biochemistry is not a moral question — it is a fact.

### 3. The right to the ADHD brain.

ADHD is not a character flaw. Not weakness. Not "the disease of today's children." It is a neurological variant that improved survival odds in evolution and is now — under 21st-century office standards — a disadvantage. Whoever has ADHD has the right to medication, to support, and to never have to **pretend to be normal** every hour of the day.

### 4. The right to heal regardless of class.

Whoever can pay the private psychiatrist, gets the medical cannabis prescription today. Whoever cannot, does not. This is **class screening**, not medical screening. Healing cannot be paywalled.

### 5. The right to privacy from the insurer and the employer.

If I take legally what the doctor prescribed, that is **none of the insurer's business**. None of my employer's. None of my neighbour's. Medical confidence, full stop. That I filled a prescription is not a verdict on me — not an HR matter, not an insurance risk.

### 6. The right to travel with my medicine.

I can cross any border with a blood-pressure pill. With a medical-cannabis capsule — from the same state, on the same prescription — I get detained at one of those borders. Customs cannot tell medicine from smuggled drugs because the paperwork is uncertain. **That is the state's failure, not the patient's.**

### 7. The right to know what I am taking.

Transparent dosage. Transparent origin. Transparent side effects. Medical cannabis works today because it is **regulated** — unlike street weed. But that is an obligation, not a favour. I should not have to "trust" my doctor blindly — let them **prove** what I am being given.

### 8. The right not to have to be ashamed of healing.

Shame does not heal. Shame retraumatises. If I receive a medicine made from a plant, that does not make me "less worthy." Let no glance say so. Let no word. Let no policy.

### 9. The right to alternatives, when the first does not work.

If medical cannabis does not help, I have the right to try another. Ketamine, psilocybin, MDMA in a therapeutic setting — as the research shows them working for PTSD, for depression. That these are "in the research phase" today does not mean they do not work. It means **we did not want them to work.**

### 10. The right to community support.

Whoever looks after their friend's dogs while that friend is in the psychiatric ward — that person is **not an accomplice**. Whoever takes their mother to the dispensary with the prescription — that person is **not a dealer**. The support network cannot be criminalised. That is the fabric of the failure.

### 11. The right to fail.

Relapse is not a sin. A bad day is not a character flaw. Whoever once got lost and gets lost again — they have not "failed" but **carry a hard illness**. The punitive system does not understand this. The healing system must.

### 12. Union: body and brain are one.

Dualism is a lie. The brain is in the body. The body is in the brain. There is no "mental illness" without physical effect. There is no physical healing without mental peace. Psychiatry is not a separate branch — it is part of medicine in full. **Body and brain are one**, and when they heal, **they heal as one.**

---

## The Oath

I swear — to myself, to my dogs, to my friends, to everyone who is starting to heal now —:

**We shall be slaves no longer.**

Not of shame.
Not of fear.
Not of hypocrisy.
Not of exhaustion.
Not of queueing for the private psychiatrist.
Not of the in-between state where the prescription is legal but the neighbour still whispers.

**The body wants to be free. And it has the right to be free.**

---

## Post-manifesto note

This is not a political programme. Not a party platform. There will be no petition, no demonstration, no referendum out of it.

This is one person who cannot sleep, and who — with the help of a poet 178 years dead — found a way to say what is wrong.

Petőfi fought for the nation. I — for my body. The dogs are asleep. The NHS prescription is on the kitchen table. Dawn is slow in coming.

**We shall be slaves no longer.**

---

**— Zoltán, Brighton, 12 April 2026, after Petőfi**
