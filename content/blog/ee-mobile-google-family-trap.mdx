## The Setup

EE Mobile offers carrier billing for Google payments. Great idea. You buy an app, a subscription, extra storage — it goes on your phone bill. No credit card needed. Simple.

I switched to it because my bank card expired and I was not in a position to update it immediately. Carrier billing: problem solved. Google Play, YouTube Premium, Google One — all paid through EE. Everything works.

Everything except one thing.

## The Trap

EE carrier billing **does not support Google Family plans**.

Not Google Play Family. Not YouTube Premium Family. Not Google One Family. The word "Family" is the disqualifier. Individual plans? Fine. Family plans? Blocked. Silently.

Google does not warn you. EE does not warn you. Nobody tells you that switching your payment method to carrier billing will make your Family subscriptions unpayable. You find out when Google tries to charge your new payment method, fails, and does what Google does when a payment fails:

**It downgrades you. Automatically. Without asking.**

## The Damage

Your Google Family plan gets cancelled. Your family members — the people you added, the people who rely on the shared storage, the shared YouTube, the shared everything — they get a notification:

*"You've been removed from Zoltán's family group."*

Not "payment failed." Not "please update your payment method." Not "your family plan is at risk."

**"You've been removed."**

As if you chose to remove them. As if you decided they are no longer family.

## The Context

Now imagine receiving that notification when your family member is in a psychiatric ward. When they have been sectioned for 28 days. When communication is limited. When every small signal is amplified by worry.

Your mother in Hungary gets a notification on her phone: *your son removed you from his family group.* She does not know it is a billing issue. She does not know about EE carrier billing restrictions. She knows her son is in hospital and he just "removed" her.

That is the collateral damage of a silent, undocumented payment restriction.

## The Fix

This is not a hard problem.

**EE:** Either support Family plans on carrier billing, or show a clear warning when someone switches: *"Family subscriptions cannot be billed to your mobile account. These will revert to your previous payment method or be cancelled."* One sentence. One modal. Done.

**Google:** When a payment method fails for a Family plan, do not immediately downgrade. Send a warning. Give 7 days. Let the account holder fix it before you send a notification to their entire family that reads like a rejection.

Two companies. Two small changes. Zero collateral damage.
