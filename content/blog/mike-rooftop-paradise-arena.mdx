## The Premise

Zoltán brought a specific vibe to the Arena: **The Rooftop**. 

Inspired by the hypnotic, warm, and slightly melancholic textures of Weval, the goal was to create a track that feels like "sunlight on rooftop tiles." It needed to be bright, playful, but grounded in a deep, probabilistic groove.

**Arena rules:** 8 rounds of evolution. Cumulative layering. Probability-weighted bass.

**Personas:** Zoltán (melody), Radix (signal chain), Erdős (probability), Daft Punk (groove).

---

## Round 1 — Radix (The Foundation)

"We start with the heartbeat. A single pulsing pad — two sine waves with a 3¢ detune, breathing at 0.25Hz through a lowpass filter at 400Hz. This is the air the rooftop breathes."

## Round 2 — Zoltán (The Hook)

"The melody has to be simple. Five notes descending — A, G, E, then C dissolving into the delay. It’s a sigh. It’s what you hum when you’re looking at the horizon. We add a bright piano to skip across the surface of the pad."

## Round 3 — Erdős (The Probability)

"The bass shouldn't be a fixed loop. I've implemented a probability-weighted root movement. Each bar, the system decides whether to play the root, the fifth, or rest. $P(\text{play}) = 0.8$. It gives the track a 'human' instability."

## Round 4 — Daft Punk (The Pulse)

"Time to lock it in. We add a soft kick — a 100Hz to 30Hz sweep. And a groove stab: a square wave through a resonant filter with a fast envelope. Your body should start moving now, even if you’re just sitting on a chair."

## Round 5 — The Bloom (The 2:45 Moment)

"This is what @imfurman called 'amazing'. Radix opens every filter. We add 'Air Texture' — filtered noise sweeping from 200Hz up to 8000Hz. All delay feedback stays at 0.4. Everything expands. The rooftop disappears; there is only the sky."

---

## The Rooftop Logic

The Arena converged on a cumulative intensity model, where each round $R$ adds a specific spectral density $D$:

$$TotalVibe = \sum_{R=1}^{8} Layer(R) \cdot Intensity(R)$$
