# Why I Still Code When AI Does It Better

![A developer smiling while typing on a mechanical keyboard, with holographic lines of code flowing around them and an AI glowing core in the background, vibrant synthwave style](https://placehold.co/800x400)

_By Zoltan Erdos | Brighton, UK | January 2026_

People ask me this question all the time: "Zoltan, AI can write code now. Why do you still do it?"

It is a fair question, especially for junior developers wondering if learning to code is still worth the effort. I ask myself the same thing sometimes when I watch Claude write in ten seconds what would take me an hour, and do it with cleaner, more elegant syntax.

So why do I still code? Let me tell you.

## Yes, AI Codes Better Than Me

I am going to say something most developers will not admit: AI writes better code than I do.

The AI does not get tired at 11pm. It does not forget edge cases because it was thinking about dinner. It has read more code than I ever will, knows patterns from millions of repositories, and remembers every best practice. I cannot compete with that, and I have stopped trying.

But here is the thing: I still code every single day. And I love it more than ever.

## The Craft Brings Me Joy

I am a big geek. I always have been. When I was young, I dreamed about building things, understanding how systems work, and making computers do what I wanted.

That feeling has not changed. When I solve a hard problem, a small spark happens in my brain. A moment of clarity. The world makes sense for a second. No AI can give me that feeling.

![A side-by-side comparison of an artist painting on canvas and a developer writing code, showing the concept of craft and process over pure output, digital illustration](https://placehold.co/600x300)

JavaScript, TypeScript, Docker, distributed systems—these are not just tools to me. They are my craft. When a painter uses a camera, they do not stop painting. The camera takes a more accurate picture, but painting is about the process: the brush in your hand, the colors mixing, the choices you make. Coding is the same for me. The act of coding is where I find joy.

## AI Taught Me More Than School Ever Did

Here is something funny: AI has made me a better developer.

When I started using AI tools, I thought they would replace my learning. Why study when you can just ask? I was so wrong. AI became the best teacher I ever had.

![A glowing roadmap labeled "Understanding" winding through a futuristic city, symbolizing the continuous journey of learning accelerated by AI, 3d render](https://placehold.co/600x300)

When the AI writes code, I ask why. Why this pattern? What happens if this fails? The AI explains patiently, without judgment. I learn things every day that would have taken me months to discover alone. AI did not replace my learning; it accelerated it. Understanding is where the real joy lives.

## Feeling Useful Matters More Than Being Efficient

Money is nice. It pays for food and a warm place to live. But money is not enough. I need to feel useful. I need a challenge and a reason to wake up in the morning that is not just a paycheck.

When I build something, I am not just generating output. I am solving real problems for real people. My project, spike.land, is my playground and my laboratory. It is not efficient—an AI could probably build it faster with fewer bugs. But efficiency is overrated. Meaning is not.

## The AI Cannot Feel What I Feel

There is something the AI will never understand: the frustration when code does not work, the late nights debugging, the moment you finally find the bug. And then the joy. The relief. The pride.

These feelings are mine. The AI writes code like water flows downhill—natural, effortless, without emotion. But I am not a machine, and the struggle is part of why I code. When I finish a hard project, I feel accomplishment and growth. The AI is the same every day; it does not grow through struggle. I do.

## Keep Coding

If you are a junior developer reading this and wondering whether to keep coding, here is my answer: **Keep coding.**

Not because you are better than AI. You are probably not. Keep coding because it makes you feel alive. Because you love the craft. Because solving problems gives you joy that no algorithm can provide.

AI is a tool. A powerful one. Use it, learn from it, and let it make you better. But do not let it take away your joy. Do not let efficiency kill your passion. 

I code because I am passionate about programming. It challenges me and makes me feel useful. AI does it better, but I do it anyway. And I have never been happier.
