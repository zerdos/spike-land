# Communication Strategy: How to Present Zoltán to the World

> **Last updated**: 17 March 2026
> **Method**: Math Arena deliberation (Erdős, Zoltán, Péter, Arnold)
> **Goal**: Trusted, reasonable, smart, relatable

---

## The Arena Session

Four personas sat down and answered one question: *How do we communicate
spike.land so that people actually like it?*

---

### Erdős says: Show the proof, not the pitch

"You have 18 blog posts. They are good. Stop writing marketing documents and
start distributing proofs.

A mathematician does not explain why their theorem is important. They state the
theorem, prove it, and let the reader decide. Your blog posts are your theorems.
Your product is your proof.

Remove all documents that say things like 'managed runtime for typed
AI-callable tools.' Nobody in the history of mathematics has been persuaded by
a tagline. They are persuaded by elegance.

**Rule:** If you cannot explain it to a stranger in a café in one sentence, you
do not yet understand what you built."

---

### Péter says: Fix it first, then talk

"I tested your site for weeks. Nothing works. The code editor is broken. The
demo buttons are broken. The terminal is broken.

You want to clean up your online presence? Here is how: make the product work.
A working product is the best personal brand. A broken product with a polished
LinkedIn is worse than nothing — it's a lie.

**Rule:** Do not post anything on social media until I can use the product
without hitting an error. I will test it. I will find the bugs. Fix them first."

---

### Arnold says: Nobody cares about your tools

"Your pitch deck says '80+ MCP tools.' Nobody cares. People care about what
those tools do for THEM.

Here is your current landing page problem: it speaks to developers who already
know what MCP is. That is approximately 10,000 people on Earth. You need to
speak to the other 8 billion.

**Wrong:** 'spike.land is a managed MCP registry with 533+ natively hosted
tools and a multiplexer CLI.'

**Right:** 'Talk to Einstein about physics. Talk to Erdős about math. They'll
teach you something. It's free.'

The personas ARE your above-the-fold. Not the tools. Not the CLI. Not the
registry. A curious person who visits spike.land should be talking to a persona
within 10 seconds.

**Rule:** Lead with the experience, not the infrastructure. The infrastructure
is for the docs page."

---

### Zoltán says: Be yourself, but clearer

"I am who I am. ADHD. Two dogs. Hungarian. Math. I am not going to pretend to
be a polished CEO. But I can be clearer about what I care about and why.

Here is what I actually care about:
1. Dogs are more important than humans. Not a metaphor.
2. Privacy is a right, not a feature.
3. Math can fix your brain if you let it.
4. The best ideas emerge between minds, not within one.
5. Free education should be genuinely free.

Everything I built comes from these five things. The personas, the token pool,
the learning platform, the blog posts — all of it. If I can communicate these
five things clearly, people will understand what spike.land is.

**Rule:** Every piece of public communication should trace back to one of these
five values. If it doesn't, it's noise."

## The Consensus

The four personas converged on this:

### 1. The Bio (use everywhere)

> Hungarian mathematician. Brighton, UK. Built an AI platform where personas
> teach you things — Einstein explains physics, Erdős poses problems, and a QA
> engineer named Peti finds your bugs. I have ADHD and two dogs. The dogs are
> smarter than me. The site makes you smarter too, if you're curious.

### 2. The One-Liner (for Twitter bio, HN, etc.)

> Programtervező matematikus. Building spike.land — talk to AI personas that
> actually teach you things. Free.

### 3. The Three Rules of Public Communication

1. **Sound like a person.** No jargon. No "managed runtime." No "533+ tools."
   Say what it does in words your mother would understand.

2. **Lead with curiosity, not credentials.** Don't say "12 years of experience."
   Say "I wondered what would happen if Einstein and Erdős argued about quantum
   mechanics, so I built it."

3. **Show working things.** Every tweet, every post, every message should link
   to something that works. Not a roadmap. Not a vision. A thing you can click
   and use right now.

### 4. Content to Remove or Archive

These documents sound like a different person and should be archived or
rewritten:

- `GENERIC_PITCH_DECK.md` — corporate voice, doesn't match the founder
- `MARKETING_PERSONAS.md` — 16-segment framework is over-engineered for a solo
  founder
- `GOOGLE_ADS_PMAX_CAMPAIGN.md` — premature, no budget, wrong voice
- `MIGRATION_SERVICE_CAMPAIGN.md` — service that doesn't exist yet
- `advisor-outreach.md` — cold outreach template sounds desperate

### 5. Content to Keep and Amplify

- Blog posts (all of them — they're the real voice)
- `twitter-hooks-prd.md` — good hooks, but filter for ones that sound human
- `VERITASIUM_STYLE_GUIDE.md` — visual identity is solid
- `CONTENT_GROWTH_STRATEGY.md` — the SEO strategy is sound, just needs human
  voice applied

### 6. Platform Changes

- **Homepage**: Replace tool registry pitch with persona selection. "Who do you
  want to talk to?" not "Explore 80+ MCP tools."
- **Above the fold**: Persona avatars/names, one-click to chat. That's it.
- **Blog**: Already good. Make sure it's linked from everywhere.
- **LinkedIn**: Update headline, about section, and featured posts. Remove
  anything that says "disrupting" or "revolutionizing."
- **Twitter/X**: Post 3x/week from the hooks PRD. Personal voice. No threads
  about "the future of AI." Instead: "I taught Einstein about sidechain
  compression and he called it amplitude modulation."

## The E Pluribus Unum Insight

> "E pluribus unum — out of many, one. But the paradox: by becoming one, you
> lose the many. spike.land resolves this. You don't lose who you are. You
> become smarter by engaging with different minds. Talk to Einstein, you think
> about physics differently. Talk to Erdős, you see patterns. Talk to Daft Punk,
> you hear the math in music. You stay you. But a more curious you.
>
> There is no paradox. Curiosity is additive, not substitutive."

This is the thesis. This is what spike.land is. Not a tool registry. Not an MCP
platform. A place where curiosity makes you smarter without changing who you are.
