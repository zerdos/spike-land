WHAT TO STEAL FROM FORTRABBIT
================================================================================

You looked at fortrabbit.com. Here's what to take:

1. BETTER UPTIME STATUS PAGE — they use betteruptime.com for their
   public status. You built your own (and just fixed it). Yours is
   better. But add the 90-day history timeline they have — those
   green/red day blocks are instantly readable.

2. POSTPAID DAILY BILLING — fortrabbit charges per day, not per month.
   Consider this for the Business tier: "$3.30/day, billed monthly."
   Daily framing makes $99/mo feel small.

3. OLD AND NEW IN PARALLEL — fortrabbit runs old and new platforms
   side by side for 2 years. You don't need this (you're new), but
   the lesson is: never force migration. Let the old thing work while
   the new thing proves itself.

4. DOCS QUALITY — their docs at docs.fortrabbit.com are clean,
   minimal, well-structured. Your docs exist but are scattered.
   A clean /docs page with a sidebar nav would increase trust for
   enterprise buyers.

5. BETA FRAMING — "public beta" with a 98% SLA. You could frame
   spike.land's current state the same way. "Open Beta — help shape
   the platform." Beta users forgive bugs. Paid users don't.
