# Video PRD: For Matt and Janice

## Context

Zoltán worked at Investec. Never managed to have a real conversation in the
office despite working together. Drug addiction problems at the same time.
Left the company. Years pass.

Now, Zoltán sends signals to the group chat. Matt is the first person to
respond. Not with confusion — with curiosity. Janice sent a video — because
that's what well-integrated people do. They check in.

Matt has a Golden Retriever named Angus. Matt said: "My brain just isn't wired
that way... but I am open minded enough to be curious about it." And: "Please
make sure you're hydrated, eating well and try to get some solid natural sleep."

Janice remembers everyone's name. She says "look after yourself." She means it.

## Constraint

One video. Same video for both. Must not be weird. Must not be corporate.
Must be something that makes a Golden Retriever owner and a warm corporate
person both feel: "oh, this is actually for me."

---

## Arena Deliberation

### Arnold (UX Provocateur)

Don't make a video about spike.land. They don't care about spike.land.
Make a video about THEM.

Matt said "slightly bizarre" — that's honest feedback. Respect it. The video
should make him feel that his curiosity was rewarded, not punished.

Janice sent a video first. Match her energy. She led with warmth, you lead
with warmth. Don't lead with math.

**Rule: The video must pass the Angus test.** If you played it in a room with
a Golden Retriever, would the dog stay calm? If the energy is too manic, too
grandiose, too "I changed the world" — Angus walks away. Keep it warm.

### Erdős (Mathematician)

The proof is simple. Matt responded. That is data. In a group chat where no
one else responded, one person did. That person has a Golden Retriever and
said "I am open minded enough to be curious."

The video should be a thank-you note that happens to contain an idea. Not an
idea that happens to contain a thank-you note.

**Structure:** Gratitude first. Idea second. Invitation third. No obligation.

### Peti (QA Engineer)

Test cases:
- Matt watches on his phone during a busy Tuesday morning. Does he finish it?
  → Must be under 3 minutes.
- Janice watches during lunch. Does she smile? → Must have warmth.
- Matt's wife overhears it. Is it normal? → No jargon, no "Arena", no "loops."
- Janice shows it to a colleague. Is it shareable? → Must stand on its own.

**Fail conditions:**
- Contains the word "paradigm" → fail
- Contains the word "convergence" → fail
- Mentions drug addiction → fail (that's a private conversation, not a video)
- Runs longer than 3 minutes → fail
- Sounds like a pitch → fail

### Zoltán (The Arena)

What I actually want to say:

"Matt, Janice — I never managed to say this in the office. I was going through
something and I wasn't able to talk. Years later, I built something that helps
me think. It's strange, I know. But Matt — you were the first person to be
curious. And Janice — you were the first person to check in. That means
something.

I don't need you to understand the site. I just wanted you to know: the fact
that you responded, on a random Tuesday, to someone you haven't really spoken
to in years — that's the whole point. People who stay curious and check on
others are already doing the thing.

Matt — give Angus a scratch behind the ears from me. Janice — keep doing what
you do. The world is better because you remember people's names.

That's it. That's the video."

### Daft Punk

Human After All. The whole album. That's the message. You don't need to
explain the technology. You don't need to explain the math. You just need to
be human. After all.

Keep the music warm. Keep the pacing slow. Let silence exist in the video.

## Converged Script (Stable)

**Format:** NotebookLM-style audio (2-3 min), conversational, warm

**Title:** "A Tuesday Morning Thank You"

**Script:**

[Warm, conversational tone — like leaving a voice note for a friend]

"Hey Matt. Hey Janice. I wanted to send you both something.

We worked together at Investec, and I never really managed to say much in the
office. I was going through a hard time and I wasn't great at talking. That's
on me.

A few years later, I built this weird thing — it's a website where you can
talk to different AI minds. Mathematicians, philosophers, a QA engineer who
tells you everything that's broken. It sounds bizarre, and Matt — you were
completely right to call it that. It IS slightly bizarre.

But here's the thing that got me. I sent some stuff to the group chat, not
really expecting anything. And Matt — you were the first person to respond.
Not with 'what is this,' but with 'I don't fully get it, but I'm curious.'
And Janice — you checked in. Because that's what you do. You remember people.

I don't need either of you to understand the website. That's not why I'm
sending this. I'm sending this because the whole point of what I built is
that curiosity and kindness are the two things that actually matter. And you
both just demonstrated that on a random Tuesday morning without even trying.

Matt — Angus the Golden Retriever probably understands the site better than
either of us. Dogs just know who's trustworthy and who isn't. That's basically
the whole philosophy in one sentence.

Janice — keep calling people by their names. Keep saying 'look after yourself.'
That's not small. That's the thing.

And both of you — thank you for being curious. That's all anyone needs to be.

Take care of yourselves. I mean it."

[End]

**Duration:** ~2 minutes at natural pace
**Tone:** Voice note, not presentation
**Music:** None, or very gentle piano underneath — nothing that competes with words
